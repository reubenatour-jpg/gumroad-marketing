## Mistake #1: Using One-Word Tags

**The problem:** Etsy gives you 13 tags. Some sellers use 6. Some use 4. Some use 13 but they're all single words like "necklace", "gift", "jewelry".

Etsy's search algorithm matches whole phrases, not individual words. A tag that says "jewelry" is almost useless because every jewellery listing has it. A tag that says "personalised birthstone jewellery" tells Etsy exactly when to show your listing.

**The fix:** Every tag should be 2-4 words. Use the exact phrases real buyers type into the search bar.

```
❌ "gift" "birthday" "jewelry" "necklace" "gold"
✅ "birthday gift for her" "gold necklace personalized" "engraved jewelry for wife" "minimalist gold pendant"
```

**Quick test:** Open Etsy in an incognito window and type what you think your tag phrase is. If autocomplete suggests your phrase, you're on the right track.

## Mistake #2: Repeating the Same Words in Every Tag

**The problem:** This is the most common mistake in the listings I analysed. Sellers repeat their main keyword in all 13 tags, thinking it reinforces it.

Etsy knows. It sees "necklace" in every single tag and simply ignores the duplicates. You've effectively wasted 10 tags.

Example of what NOT to do:

```
Tag 1: "gold necklace"
Tag 2: "necklace for her"
Tag 3: "personalised necklace"
Tag 4: "gold jewellery necklace"
```

Every single tag has "necklace" or "gold". Etsy only needs to see these words once.

**The fix:** Write all your tags, then scan for repeated words. Each word should appear in no more than 2-3 tags.

```
Tag 1: "gold pendant necklace"
Tag 2: "meaningful birthday gift"
Tag 3: "personalised jewellery for her"
Tag 4: "engraved keepsake jewellery"
Tag 5: "minimalist dainty pendant"
Tag 6: "anniversary gift wife"
```

Now "necklace" appears in tags 1 and 5. "Jewellery" in 3 and 4. Everything else introduces new search terms. Your 13 tags now cover 25+ unique search terms instead of 3.

## Mistake #3: Keyword-Stuffed Titles

**The problem:** In an attempt to rank for everything, sellers write titles like:

> "Custom necklace gift for her birthday personalised jewellery gold pendant for women wife girlfriend mom"

This doesn't work. Etsy's algorithm has gotten smart enough to recognise keyword stuffing. More importantly, **real buyers** see this and scroll past. It looks spammy.

**The fix:** Write titles for humans first, search engines second. Use this formula:

> **[Product Name] | [Feature] | [Occasion/Recipient] | [Style/Material]**

> "Personalised Birthstone Necklace | Custom Engraved Gold Pendant | Meaningful Birthday Gift for Her | Minimalist Dainty Jewellery"

Read it out loud. Does it sound like something a person would say? If yes, you're good.

## Mistake #4: Ignoring the First 160 Characters of Your Description

**The problem:** Etsy shows the first ~160 characters of your description in search results (above the fold) and in Google snippets. Most sellers waste this prime real estate on fluff.

Here's what a lot of listings start with:

> "Welcome to my shop! Thank you for stopping by. Please read the full description before purchasing. All items are made with love..."

That's 160 characters burned on nothing.

**The fix:** The first 160 characters should answer the buyer's core question — "what is this and why should I care?"

> "A handcrafted sterling silver pendant featuring your loved one's birthstone. Each piece is custom-engraved and arrives in gift-ready packaging — perfect for birthdays, anniversaries, or just because."

Now when someone finds your listing on Google, the snippet actually sells the product.

## Mistake #5: Not Using All 13 Tags

**The problem:** This sounds obvious, but my analysis found that nearly 40% of listings use fewer than 13 tags. Even 11 tags is leaving search traffic on the table.

**Why it matters:** Each tag is another doorway into your listing. Every missing tag means Etsy has fewer ways to match your product to a buyer's search.

**The fix:** Fill all 13. Every time. Before publishing, count your tags. If you're stuck on the last few, think about:
- The material ("sterling silver", "recycled gold")
- The style ("minimalist", "boho", "rustic")
- The recipient ("gift for dad", "gift for girlfriend")
- The occasion ("wedding gift", "Christmas present")
- The use case ("everyday wear", "stacking ring")

---

## Mistake #6: Identical Tags Across All Your Listings

**The problem:** Many sellers find 13 tags that work and copy-paste them to every listing.

"Gift for her" works for one necklace. It doesn't work for your resin coasters. Etsy sees the same tags across multiple products and gets confused about which listing is relevant for which search.

**The fix:** Each listing should have tags specific to that product. Yes, there will be some overlap (shop-wide terms like "handmade", "gift", your shop name). But at least 8-10 tags should be unique to each listing.

---

## Mistake #7: Setting and Forgetting

**The problem:** You optimised your listings once, six months ago, and haven't touched them since. The most successful Etsy sellers treat SEO as ongoing maintenance, not a one-time setup.

**What changes:**
- Search trends shift (what people searched in December is different from June)
- Competitor tags evolve
- Etsy's algorithm updates
- Your product lineup grows

**The fix:** Every 3 months, do a 15-minute SEO audit on your top 10 listings:
1. Check if your keywords still match search trends
2. Look at competitor listings for new tag ideas
3. Update descriptions with seasonal and fresh language
4. Check Etsy search analytics (if you have Etsy Plus or Etsy Ads data)

---

## The 15-Minute SEO Fix

Don't have time to redo everything? Here's the minimum effective dose:

1. **Fix your tags** — remove repeated words, make every tag 2-4 words
2. **Rewrite your first 160 characters** — make it a mini-product description
3. **Clean up your titles** — remove stuffing, add pipe separators
4. **Fill all 13 tags** — no excuses

That's 15 minutes per listing. Do it for your top 5 listings and you'll see movement within a week.
