## What Makes an Etsy Listing Convert?

Etsy's algorithm is a matchmaker. It wants to show the right product to the right person. But once a buyer clicks on your listing, the algorithm steps aside — and your writing has to close the deal.

Top-performing listings nail three things:

1. **Searchability** — Etsy finds your listing
2. **Scannability** — The buyer quickly understands what you're offering
3. **Persuasiveness** — The copy removes doubt and triggers the purchase

Most sellers only focus on #1. The best sellers nail all three.

## Step 1: Write Titles That Etsy (and Humans) Love

Your title serves two masters: Etsy's search algorithm and the scrolling buyer.

**The mistake most sellers make:** Stuffing keywords into a run-on sentence that reads like a robot wrote it.

**The fix:** The "Hook + Descriptor + Keyword" formula.

```
Bad: "Custom necklace jewelry gift for her birthday"
Good: "Personalised Birthstone Necklace | Minimal Gold Jewellery | Meaningful Gift for Her | Custom Engraved Pendant"
```

See the difference? The second one reads naturally, tells the buyer exactly what they're getting, and still includes plenty of search terms.

**Pattern from the top 500 listings:**
- Start with the product name (what is it)
- Add key features (material, style, personalisation)
- End with the occasion or recipient (who is it for)
- Use pipe symbols (|) to separate sections — Etsy reads them as natural breaks

## Step 2: Write Descriptions That Answer the Unspoken Questions

When a buyer reads your description, they're asking questions they might not say out loud:

- Will this actually look good in my home?
- Is it good quality?
- Will it arrive on time?
- What if I don't like it?
- Is this worth the price?

Your description needs to answer every single one.

**The structure top sellers use:**

1. **The opener (2-3 sentences)** — Paint a picture of the product in use
2. **The details** — Materials, dimensions, colours, customisation options
3. **The proof** — Social proof, guarantees, shipping info
4. **The close** — A gentle nudge toward checkout

Here's a real example of a description opener from a top-selling listing:

> "Imagine walking into your living room and seeing this warm, hand-thrown ceramic vase on your coffee table — filled with dried eucalyptus, catching the afternoon light. That's the feeling this piece was made for."

That's not fluff. It's visualisation. The buyer isn't just buying a vase — they're buying the feeling of having it in their home.

**Your turn:** Start every product description by describing the *result* the buyer wants, not the *features* of the product.

## Step 3: Tags Are Not Optional

Etsy gives you 13 tags. Use all 13.

But here's what's interesting from my analysis: the top 500 listings don't just use any 13 tags. They use a **tag ladder**:

- **3 broad tags** (e.g. "gift for her", "home decor")
- **5 specific tags** (e.g. "ceramic vase", "handthrown pottery")
- **3 long-tail tags** (e.g. "rustic living room decor")
- **2 occasion tags** (e.g. "housewarming gift", "birthday present")

This ladder means you get found by Etsy's algorithm at multiple levels — broad enough for discovery, specific enough for targeted searches.

## Step 4: Make Your Listing Scannable

Nobody reads an Etsy listing like a novel. They scan in an F-pattern — top to bottom, left to right.

**What this means for your descriptions:**
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bullet points for features
- Bold key phrases
- White space everywhere

Compare:

```
This beautiful handmade necklace is made from sterling silver and features a genuine turquoise gemstone that has been ethically sourced from the American Southwest and hand-selected by our team of expert gemologists...
```

vs:

```
**Sterling Silver Turquoise Pendant**

What you're getting:
• Hand-selected turquoise from the American Southwest
• .925 sterling silver chain (adjustable 16-20")
• Ethical & sustainably sourced materials
• Gift-ready packaging included
• Free shipping to the US & Canada
```

The second version takes 3 seconds to scan. The first takes 20 seconds to parse. In mobile browsing (where 60%+ of Etsy sales happen), scannability isn't optional.

## Step 5: Use the "Because" Principle

There's a psychological trigger that top-performing listings use over and over: the word "because."

Research in behavioural science shows that people are more likely to comply with a request when you give them a reason — even a simple one. Etsy listings that explain the "why" behind their price, materials, or production process consistently outperform those that don't.

**Examples:**
- "Priced at £45 because each piece is hand-carved and takes 4 hours to complete"
- "Free shipping because we believe in no-surprise checkouts"
- "Made-to-order because every piece is custom to your measurements"

The "because" reframes features as benefits. It removes doubt.

## Quick Checklist Before You Publish

Before you hit save on your next listing, run through this:

- [ ] Title uses the Hook + Descriptor + Keyword formula
- [ ] First sentence describes the result, not the features
- [ ] Description answers: quality, delivery, fit, returns
- [ ] All 13 tags filled with a balanced ladder
- [ ] Bullet points or short paragraphs for mobile reading
- [ ] At least one "because" statement
- [ ] A clear call-to-action at the bottom
